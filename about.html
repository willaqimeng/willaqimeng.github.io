Willa is an actor, writer, director, and visual artist from New York City. Growing up with many identities - adopted, Chinese but not, American but not, Jewish, Buddhist, Quaker but not (and so on) was weird, lonely, and wonderfully inspiring. A need for creative outlets led her to drawing, painting, writing, and acting through elementary and high school.

For college, she attended Barnard and graduated with honors and a BA in English in 2020. Her fiction has won the Axinn Foundation / Anna Quindlen Award for Creative Writing and been published in the Asian American Writers' Workshop.

She is now based in Los Angeles earning an MFA in Film & TV Production at USC's School of Cinematic Arts, where her experiences in various aspects of production greatly enriched her view as a filmmaker and artist.

Her stories explore artistic passion, the meaning of dreams, Asian American identity, feminism, and the nuances of adoption.
