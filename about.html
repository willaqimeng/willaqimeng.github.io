Willa is an actor, writer, director, and visual artist from New York.

She graduated from Barnard in 2020 with a BA in English. Her senior thesis won the Axinn Foundation / Anna Quindlen Award for Creative Writing and her creative non-fiction has been published in the Asian American Writers' Workshop. While studying English at Barnard, she worked for two years at the Athena Film Festival and was a costume designer for the Barnard theatre department. Off-campus, she spent time painting, charcoaling, and developing her own short stories.

She is now based in Los Angeles earning an MFA in Film & TV Production at USC's School of Cinematic Arts, where she wrote and directed her first short, completed her first feature screenplay, and trained in production design, editing, and sound design. Her experiences in various key production roles continue to expand her view as a filmmaker and artist. She has interned with the Sundance Institute and evaluated scripts for the Feature Lab and Intensive.

With a bent towards coming-of-age and fantasy genres, her stories explore Asian American identity, dreams, feminism, transracial adoption, and artistic passion.
